## Program 1: Entertainment Development Program

### Mission
Create world-class entertainment content — films, music, interactive experiences — that inspires curiosity about AI, space, and the diversity of life across the universe.

### Sub-Programs

#### 1A. Feature Film Development
| Stage | Description | Timeline |
|---|---|---|
| Development | Script, concept, pitch deck creation | 6–12 months |
| Pre-Production | Casting, storyboards, budgeting | 3–6 months |
| Production | Principal photography / animation | 3–12 months |
| Post-Production | Editing, VFX, sound design, scoring | 3–6 months |
| Distribution | Festival circuit, streaming, theatrical | 6–18 months |

**Current Projects in Development:**
- The Quantum Zoo (family sci-fi adventure)
- Dark Matter Menagerie (adult sci-fi thriller)
- First Contact Protocol (dramatic epic)

#### 1B. Music & Visual Album Program
- Commission AI-assisted original scores and soundtracks
- Partner with independent artists to create visual albums
- Distribute through major streaming platforms and physical media
- Host live cinema events for album premieres

#### 1C. Interactive Entertainment
- Develop companion apps with AR/VR experiences for each major release
- Create browser-based games set in the Intergalactic Zoo universe
- Build immersive museum/installation experiences in partnership with science centers

#### 1D. Live Events & Experiences
- Annual "Intergalactic Film Festival" (virtual and in-person)
- Live orchestral performances of original scores
- Convention presence at San Diego Comic-Con, Dragon Con, PAX

## Program 2: Education Development Program

### Mission
Use the power of storytelling, AI, and open-source technology to make science, technology, and the wonder of life accessible to learners of all ages and backgrounds.

### Sub-Programs

#### 2A. Curriculum Integration Program
**Description:** Partner with K-12 schools and universities to integrate network content into STEM and humanities curricula.

**Course Modules Available:**
| Module | Grade Level | Subject Area |
|---|---|---|
| Intro to AI: The Zoological Curator | Grades 6–8 | Computer Science |
| Quantum Thinking: Beyond Binary | Grades 9–12 | Physics / CS |
| Species of the Cosmos: Speculative Astrobiology | University | Biology / Astronomy |
| The Ethics of Alien Contact | University | Philosophy / Ethics |
| Storytelling with AI | Grades 6–12 | English / Media Arts |
| Documentary Filmmaking for Science | University | Film / Science Communication |

**Teacher Resources Provided:**
- Lesson plans aligned to NGSS and Common Core
- Video content with classroom discussion guides
- Access to the open-source AI tools for hands-on projects
- Professional development workshops for teachers

#### 2B. Open Learning Platform
**Description:** A free online learning platform where anyone can explore the Intergalactic Zoo universe while learning real science and technology skills.

**Platform Features:**
- Self-paced courses with video lessons
- Interactive coding exercises using real AI tools
- Quizzes and project-based assessments
- Certificates of completion
- Multilingual support (target: 20+ languages)

**Course Catalog:**
1. Introduction to AI and Machine Learning
2. How to Catalog a Species: Field Biology meets AI
3. Quantum Computing for Beginners
4. Science Communication and Storytelling
5. Documentary Filmmaking: From Concept to Screen
6. Building Open-Source Software

#### 2C. Youth Mentorship Program
**Description:** Connect young people (ages 14–24) with scientists, engineers, and filmmakers from the network for mentorship and career guidance.

**Structure:**
- 6-month cohorts of 50 mentees matched with 25 mentors
- Monthly 1:1 sessions plus group workshops
- Capstone project showcased at the annual Intergalactic Film Festival
- Internship pathways for top performers

#### 2D. Educator Grant Program
**Description:** Award annual grants to teachers who create innovative lessons using the network's open-source tools.

| Grant Tier | Amount | Recipients/Year |
|---|---|---|
| Seed Grant | $500 | 50 |
| Innovation Grant | $2,500 | 20 |
| Flagship Grant | $10,000 | 5 |

## Program 3: Business Technology Development Program

### Mission
Build and commercialize AI-powered tools and platforms that serve real business needs in the media, entertainment, science, and technology sectors.

### Sub-Programs

#### 3A. AI Content Tools Suite
**Description:** A suite of open-source and commercial AI tools for content creators, scientists, and business teams.

| Tool | Description | Primary Users |
|---|---|---|
| ZooCatalog AI | AI species identification and cataloging tool | Scientists, educators |
| StoryForge AI | AI-assisted screenplay and narrative development | Writers, filmmakers |
| CampaignIQ | AI-powered marketing campaign planner | Marketers, businesses |
| DocuSync | Automated documentary research and fact-checking | Journalists, filmmakers |
| QuantumComms | Quantum-encrypted communication protocol | Enterprise, government |
| MediaMatrix | Multi-platform content distribution optimizer | Media companies |

#### 3B. API & Developer Platform
**Description:** Expose the network's AI capabilities as developer APIs for third-party integration.

**Offerings:**
- REST APIs for species identification, content analysis, and narrative generation
- SDKs for Python, JavaScript, Go, and Rust
- Sandbox environment for testing
- Developer portal with documentation and tutorials
- Tiered pricing: Free (rate-limited), Pro, Enterprise

#### 3C. Enterprise Solutions Program
**Description:** Tailored AI and multimedia solutions for enterprise clients.

**Service Packages:**
| Package | Description | Target Client |
|---|---|---|
| MediaAI Starter | AI content tools + basic support | Small studios, indie creators |
| Science Suite | Zoological and research AI tools | Universities, research labs |
| Enterprise Studio | Full custom AI multimedia platform | Major studios, broadcasters |
| Government Science | Secure, compliant research tools | Government agencies |

**Sales Process:**
1. Discovery call (understanding client needs)
2. Demo (tailored to their use case)
3. Pilot (30-day limited deployment)
4. Proposal (custom SLA, pricing, timeline)
5. Contract and onboarding

#### 3D. Startup Incubator Program
**Description:** Support early-stage startups building in the AI, media, or space technology space.

**Program Details:**
- 12-week accelerator cohorts (virtual + in-person intensive)
- Up to $50,000 in seed investment per cohort company
- Access to network's tools, APIs, and mentor network
- Demo Day at the end of each cohort
- Target: 10 companies per cohort, 2 cohorts per year

**Selection Criteria:**
- Building at the intersection of AI, media, science, or space
- Strong founding team with domain expertise
- Clear problem and early traction
- Commitment to open-source principles

## Program 4: Cross-Domain Integration Projects

### "Universe in a Frame" — Art × Science × Technology Initiative
**Description:** Annual project commissioning artists, scientists, and engineers to collaboratively create multimedia works that bridge disciplines.

**Output Examples:**
- A scientist and filmmaker co-create a documentary on astrobiology
- An AI researcher and musician co-create an AI-composed album
- An educator and game designer co-create an interactive learning experience

### "Open Archive" — Digital Preservation Program
**Description:** Use AI to digitize, catalog, and preserve historical scientific and cultural records, making them freely accessible.

**Focus Areas:**
- Digitized natural history museum archives
- Historical space mission recordings and data
- Indigenous ecological knowledge preservation
- Early cinema and documentary film restoration
